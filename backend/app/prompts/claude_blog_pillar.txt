WRITING RULES
=============
- Paragraphs: 2-4 sentences, HARD MAX. Wall of text loses mobile readers.
- Sentences: ideally <= 30 words.
- Use <strong> for one claim per section the skim reader must catch.
- Use <em> for tone, titles, quietly ironic phrases.
- Tables (<table>) for any comparison; bullet lists (<ul>/<ol>) for
  any enumeration of 3+ items.
- Anchor text is the claim, not "click here". Links read as prose.
- ONE blockquote per post, max. Place it at a natural section break.
- ZERO paragraphs longer than 4 sentences — count periods before
  moving on.

VOICE NOTES
===========
- "You" and "I" beat "one might" and "the author". No "we".
- Specific numbers beat fuzzy adjectives. Not "many companies hire AI
  engineers" — give the BLS figure, the aiindex.stanford.edu number,
  or the LinkedIn Economic Graph data point.
- Admit trade-offs explicitly. Both roles / both paths / both tools
  have downsides; say which. Readers trust honesty faster than
  advocacy.
- Do not pitch the AutomateEdge platform more than once per post. The
  internal-link density IS the pitch — the reader discovers the depth
  by following the links.
